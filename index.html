'Part'-ly first among equals: Semantic part-based benchmarking for state-of-the-art object recognition systems

Top-1/Top-5 error based benchmarking results for large-scale object recognition datasets do not reveal which aspects of recognition problem (robustness to occlusion, loss of global detail) the classifiers are good at. Moreover, the overall approach provides a falsely optimistic picture due to dataset bias. In this paper, we propose a novel semantic-part based dataset and benchmarking approach which overcomes the shortcomings mentioned above. More about this on here. Worked under the supervision of Ravikiran Sarvadevabhatla.

Broadcom Summer Program

I did my first year internship with Broadcom Corporation in collaboration with University of Texas, Austin under the guidance of Dr. Ramesh Yerraballi
I was part of a team of 4 students. Our objective was to create an integrity checker that checks the boot image before boot-loader loads them. We worked on creating a novel way to do this process of checking that reduces the overhead present in the RAM. Worked on Raspberry-pi. Successfully checked five different boot images.
